Notes to the financial statements

Note 1 — Significant accounting policies

The financial statements are prepared in accordance with International Public Sector Accounting Standards (IPSAS) on the accrual basis, using the historical cost convention except where stated. The Centre's functional currency is the US dollar.

Significant judgements and estimates have been made in respect of the useful lives of property, plant and equipment, the recoverable amount of intangible assets, the discount rate used in the valuation of post-employment benefit obligations, and provisions for litigation and similar items.

Note 2 — Revenue recognition policy

Revenue from services is recognised when the Centre has the right to receive payment from the partner organisation under the relevant Service Level Agreement and the related performance obligations have been substantively satisfied. Where multi-year service agreements are signed, revenue is recognised in the period the agreement is executed and invoiced.

Revenue from hosting arrangements is recognised over the period the hosted services are provided.

Note 11 — Consulting and contractors

Consulting and contractor expenditure includes professional fees paid to specialist consultants supporting service delivery, programme management, and governance. All contracts are awarded under the Centre's procurement policy following the standard competitive sourcing process.

Note 12 — Related party transactions

The Centre's principal related party is the World Health Organization (WHO), which acts as host organisation under a hosting MOU. WHO provides HR, payroll and selected administrative services for which it charges an administrative support fee equal to 8% of the Centre's staff costs ($5,470,000 for 20X5; 20X4: $4,970,000). Amounts payable to WHO at year end are included in accounts payable.

Key management personnel (Director, Chiefs of Division) compensation totalled $1,820,000 (20X4: $1,760,000), comprising salary, post-adjustment, allowances and pension contributions in line with UN common system rules.

No other related party transactions are reported for the current period.
