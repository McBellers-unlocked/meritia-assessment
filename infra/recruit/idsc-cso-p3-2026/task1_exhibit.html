Executive summary

March 20X6 was a strong month for the Cyber Security Operations Section. Key highlights:

  • Mean Time to Respond (MTTR) improved to 35 minutes, down from the Q4 20X5 average of 42 minutes.
  • Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) improved by 18% this month.
  • Detection volume up 42% year-on-year, reflecting continued maturity of the SOC's monitoring estate.
  • Zero critical security incidents for the third consecutive month.
  • 70% of Tier 1 alerts now auto-triaged by SOAR playbooks, releasing analyst capacity.
  • Tier-to-tier escalations remain stable and within expected ranges.
  • All identified risks are being actively mitigated under the Q1 roadmap.

The SOC onboarded two further UN-system partner agencies in March (URRSO, UNFSS). Coverage was extended without disruption to existing service levels.

1. Detection volume and coverage

The SOC processed an average of 28,400 detection events per day in March, a 42% increase year-on-year. The increase reflects continued enrichment of the detection rule library and expanded telemetry sources.

7. Continuous improvement initiatives

The following initiatives progressed in March in line with the Q1 workplan:

  • SOAR playbook expansion — four new playbooks for cloud workload events released to production.
  • Rule library review cadence — quarterly review cycle continues; next scheduled review Q2 20X6.
  • Partner onboarding — URRSO and UNFSS integrated into the SOC monitoring estate.
  • Detection engineering — three new informational-level detections deployed for identity-anomaly correlation.

8. Outlook

The SOC enters Q2 with strong momentum. Detection coverage continues to expand, response times continue to decrease, and the SOAR automation programme is on track. All identified risks are being actively mitigated under the Q1 roadmap (see Annex B). No material risk changes to report this month.
