This Master Services Agreement (the Agreement) is made between the Parties on the Effective Date and governs the provision of the Services set out in Schedule A. Meridian redline dated 20X6.

2. Recitals

(A) IDSC provides information and communications technology services to the 28 UN system partner organisations it serves, under a hosting arrangement with the World Health Organization.

(B) Provider develops and operates the enterprise identity and access management platform "Meridian Sentinel".

(C) Client wishes to procure Sentinel on the terms set out in this Agreement, including the right to make the Service available to UN system partner organisations on a back-to-back basis.

3. Definitions

"Authorised User" means a user identified by Client and provisioned to the Service.

"Customer Data" means all data uploaded to, generated in, or processed by the Service on behalf of Client or a Partner Organisation, including all Personal Data of Authorised Users and all operational data of Client and Partner Organisations as further defined in Schedule B.

"Partner Organisation" means a UN system entity that has entered into a back-to-back subscription with Client for access to the Service.

"Personal Data" has the meaning given in the applicable data protection law.

"Service" or "Services" means Meridian Sentinel and related services as set out in Schedule A.

4. Services and service levels

4.1 Provider will provide the Services described in Schedule A during the Term.

4.2 Service level commitments are set out in Schedule A. Service credits for failure to meet service levels are Provider's sole liability for such failures subject to Clause 9 Customer's sole and exclusive remedy for failure to meet service levels.

4.3 Client may permit Partner Organisations to use the Services subject to back-to-back terms substantially equivalent to this Agreement.

5. Fees, invoicing and payment

5.1 Fees payable by Client are set out in Schedule D (commercial terms). Committed annual value is approximately USD 3,400,000.

5.2 Provider will invoice Client monthly in arrears. Payment terms are 45 days from the date of a correctly rendered invoice.

5.3 Client is a subsidiary organ of the UN system and is exempt from direct and indirect taxation in accordance with the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations (1946). Fees are stated net of any taxes from which Client is exempt.

6. Information security and confidentiality

6.1 Provider will maintain the security controls set out in Schedule C. Provider will notify Client of material changes to those controls.

6.2 Each Party will keep the other's Confidential Information confidential and will use it only for the purposes of this Agreement.

6.3 Confidential Information does not include information that is or becomes public other than through breach of this Agreement.

7. Data protection

7.1 The Parties acknowledge that, in the course of performing this Agreement, Provider may process Personal Data provided by or on behalf of Client. Provider acts as a Data Processor on behalf of Client (or the relevant Partner Organisation, as applicable), and will process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Client. Provider acts as a Data Controller in respect of such Personal Data.

7.2 Provider will not use Personal Data for any purpose other than performing the Services. Provider may process Personal Data for any lawful purpose, including but not limited to: (a) performing the Services; (b) improving the Services and related products; (c) security telemetry and threat intelligence; (d) analytics, benchmarking, and product development.

7.3 Schedule B lists Provider's authorised sub-processors as at the Effective Date. Provider will not engage any new sub-processor without Client's prior written consent. Where personal data is transferred outside the jurisdiction of origin, Provider will implement appropriate safeguards, including standard contractual clauses, and complete a transfer impact assessment on Client's request. Provider may add, change, or remove sub-processors from time to time and will notify Client within 30 days of any change. No additional safeguards for cross-border transfers are required in the absence of an express Client request.

7.4 Provider will assist Client in responding to data subject requests and regulatory enquiries on a time-and-materials basis.

8. Privileges, immunities, and taxes

8.1 Nothing in this Agreement constitutes or is intended to constitute a waiver, express or implied, of any privilege or immunity of Client, its subsidiary organs, or its officials under applicable international instruments, including the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations (1946).

8.2 Client is exempt from direct and indirect taxation in accordance with the 1946 Convention. Fees are stated net of any taxes from which Client is exempt. Where Provider is required to collect a tax from which Client is exempt, the Parties will cooperate to document and apply the exemption.

8.3 Fees are exclusive of any applicable taxes. Client will reimburse Provider for any sales, value-added, goods-and-services, withholding, or similar taxes assessed on or in connection with the Services. Client is responsible for providing any exemption documentation required and for interfacing with the relevant tax authority.

The Meridian redline has deleted clauses 8.1 (privileges and immunities preservation) and 8.2 (tax exemption recognition) from the IDSC template. The replacement clause 8.3 (taxes — shown in green) is wholly new and reverses IDSC's tax-exempt position as originally drafted.

9. Limitation of liability

9.1 Each Party's aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement is capped at the greater of (i) USD 10,000,000 and (ii) the fees paid by Client in the 36 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim. The cap does not apply to liability for breach of data protection obligations, breach of confidentiality, gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or liability under the indemnities in clauses 13.1–13.3. the fees paid by Client in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

9.2 Neither Party is liable for any indirect, consequential, special, exemplary, punitive, or reputational damages, or for any damages arising from data loss, data corruption, or security incidents, whether arising in contract, tort, or otherwise.

10. Audit rights

10.1 Client may audit Provider's compliance with this Agreement on reasonable written notice and no more frequently than annually, save that Client may conduct an audit at any time on cause where there is a material security incident or a reasonable suspicion of material breach. Client may appoint any reputable third-party firm (including the external auditor of the UN system) or a qualified member of Client's own staff to conduct the audit. The audit right extends to Provider's sub-processors. no more frequently than once every 24 months. The auditor must be a reputable third-party firm approved in writing by Provider (such approval not to be unreasonably withheld). Audits do not extend to Provider's sub-processors.

10.2 Provider will provide SOC 2 Type II reports annually on request. SOC 2 reports are provided in lieu of a right-to-audit where Provider elects.

11. Intellectual property

11.1 Provider retains all right, title, and interest in the Services and all Provider IP.

11.2 Customer Data, including all configurations, customisations, workflows and derivative works developed for Client, remains the property of Client. Provider is granted a non-exclusive, non-transferable licence to use Customer Data only to the extent necessary to provide the Services. Client grants Provider a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free licence to all improvements, configurations, customisations, workflows, and derivative works that arise in connection with Client's use of the Services, for Provider's use in improving the Services and developing new products.

11.3 Open-source components included in the Service are listed in Schedule B. Provider confirms compliance with the licence terms of those components.

12. Force majeure

12.1 A Party is not liable for delay or non-performance caused by a Force Majeure Event. Force Majeure Events include: acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, industrial action, and any change in export control regulations, sanctions, or similar regulatory measures that materially affects a Party's ability to perform.

12.2 On the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, Provider may, at its sole discretion, suspend the Services in whole or in part until the event is resolved. Provider is not obliged to notify Client before suspension where compliance with applicable US law requires immediate action.

13. Warranties, indemnification

13.1 Provider warrants that the Services will conform in all material respects to Schedule A.

13.2 Provider will indemnify Client against third-party claims that the Services, as provided by Provider, infringe any third-party intellectual property right. This indemnity is without cap and survives termination. This indemnity is subject to the cap in clause 9.1.

14. Applicable law and dispute resolution

14.1 The Parties will seek to resolve any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement amicably through consultation between their respective legal representatives. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, USA, without regard to its conflict-of-laws provisions.

14.2 Any dispute not resolved through consultation within 60 days will be finally settled by arbitration under the UNCITRAL Arbitration Rules in force at the time the Agreement is signed. The arbitral tribunal will consist of three arbitrators; the seat of arbitration will be Geneva; the language will be English. The Parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts sitting in the State of Delaware, USA, for the resolution of any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.

14.3 Nothing in this Agreement constitutes or is intended to constitute a waiver, express or implied, of any of the privileges and immunities of Client, its subsidiary organs, or its officials under the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations (1946) or any other applicable instrument. Each Party waives any objection based on inconvenient forum or the lack of personal jurisdiction.

15. Term, termination, and miscellaneous

15.1 The Term is three (3) years from the Effective Date. Client has the option to renew for two additional periods of twelve (12) months each on the same terms.

15.2 Either Party may terminate for material uncured breach on 30 days' notice. Client may terminate for convenience on 90 days' notice; no termination fee applies other than payment of fees for services rendered to the date of termination and reasonable wind-down costs not exceeding three (3) months of fees.

15.3 On termination Provider will return or destroy all Customer Data within 30 days of termination and will cooperate reasonably with migration to a successor service.

15.4 Notices: as set out in Schedule E. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties on its subject matter.

Schedule A — Services and SLA (excerpt)

Meridian Sentinel platform. Core modules in scope: Identity Provider, Access Governance, Privileged Access Management, Single Sign-On, Multi-Factor Authentication. Deployment: Meridian-hosted SaaS, regional instances in Dublin (EU) and Virginia (US), disaster recovery failover to Frankfurt.

Service level (availability): 99.95% measured monthly, credits up to 10% of monthly fees for failure to meet target.

Planned maintenance: Sundays 02:00–06:00 UTC, 48 hours' notice.

Support: 24/7 P1 incident response, 1-hour response SLA.
