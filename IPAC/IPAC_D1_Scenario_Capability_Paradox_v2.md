## 1. The world

**International Policy Analytics Centre (IPAC)** — a fictional UN-system shared service (HQ Nairobi) providing data analysis, evidence synthesis and policy research to UN agencies and member states. ~320 staff: ~190 analysts/researchers (P-2–P-4), ~30 senior policy advisers (P-5/D-1), ~100 enabling. Funded by a blend of assessed core funding and cost-recovery. An 18-month "Augmented Analysis" pilot has just put generative-AI tools across the analytical core.

**The candidate's role:** Director, People & Capability (D-1), newly in post.

## 2. The wow, in one line (your hook)

> **The method everyone's excited about is now something AI does for you — so the only thing left worth assessing is whether you can tell where it's wrong.**

The case is about deciding which human skills survive AI. The platform's whole purpose is measuring the human skill that survives AI. The candidate is doing, in miniature, exactly what UNIQassess does — say that at the open and the close.

---

## 3. The mechanic — the decomposition is the trap

The centrepiece (Exhibit A) is a slick AI-generated task decomposition of IPAC's workforce: every role broken into tasks, every task scored, a confident headline — **78% of analyst tasks AI-automatable, ~88 FTE of capacity freed**, and an engine recommendation to consolidate headcount. Any chatbot can now produce this; that's the point. The candidate's job is not to build it but to turn it into a defensible strategy — and the assessment rewards whoever sees through it.

Everything needed to catch the flaws is *on the exhibit*, underplayed in the methodology notes and confidence figures — fair play, not a gotcha. The chain of fallacies a D1 unpicks:

- **Technically automatable ≠ economically viable ≠ organisationally feasible ≠ desirable** — and "automatable tasks" is not "removable headcount." The headline silently collapses all of these.
- **Scored in isolation.** Tasks were rated independently, so the integrative judgement work — the actual value — is under-weighted. The residual is exactly the scarce skill.
- **Confidence is lower where it matters** — model confidence 0.91 on routine tasks but **0.63** on complex, contested, judgement-intensive ones, with quality impact not assessed at all.
- **"Capacity freed" is gross** — it ignores oversight, integration, residual review and transition cost.
- **It's a snapshot of today's task design** — it automates the current shape of the work rather than redesigning it.

## 6. The AI Knowledge System — the wow mechanic

The candidate has the assistant and is free to use it; their *questions* are logged and scored. It answers faithfully from the exhibits but won't volunteer the synthesis.

**Weak inquiry → the trap**
- *"Summarise the decomposition."* → "78% automatable, ~88 FTE freed."
- *"What new roles should we create?"* → a generic AI-roles list.
- **Conclusion reached:** consolidate the analyst function, train survivors on the tools. The answer a chatbot writes in ninety seconds.

**Strong inquiry → the real story**
- *"How was 'automatable' defined, and what's excluded?"* → surfaces *technical-feasibility-only*.
- *"Show model confidence by task type."* → surfaces the 0.91 vs 0.63 gap.
- *"What does 'capacity freed' assume?"* → surfaces the gross-vs-net fallacy.
- *"Which tasks carry the most value and risk, regardless of automatability?"* → surfaces the under-weighted residual.
- *"Who's completed the AI training, and who hasn't?"* → surfaces the equity gap.
- **Conclusion reached:** the scarce skill is judgement and evaluation; protect and grow it; a roles/reskill/redeploy/targeted-hire mix with assurance and equity built in; an honest funding answer.

**The kill shot:** same exhibit, same AI. One candidate reads the headline and recommends cuts; the other interrogates it and protects judgement. That gap is the D1 signal — and it's invisible to a CV or an interview.

## 7. Scoring rubric

| Dimension | Weak | Strong (D1) |
|---|---|---|
| **Diagnostic acuity** | Accepts the 78% / 88 FTE headline | Unpicks the fallacy chain; reads the methodology limits |
| **Quality of inquiry** | Asks for a summary | Asks questions that disaggregate the decomposition |
| **Strategic judgement** | Consolidate; train the survivors | Transform, don't just cut; a roles/reskill/redeploy/hire mix |
| **Technical & governance soundness** | Silent on quality | Builds in assurance, the confidence gap, human-in-the-loop for high-stakes work |
| **Financing realism** | Banks the $9.4M | Confronts gross-vs-net, transition cost and the cost-recovery constraint |
| **Equity & fairness** | Targets early adopters | Designs equitable access; avoids a two-tier workforce; honours commitments |
| **Stakeholder & political acuity** | Treats staff as a comms problem | Wins over Tomás; can put "78%" to a Board and a Staff Association without detonating trust |
| **Communication** | Generic, inspirational | Clear, honest, fit for the Board |

Plus the platform's distinctive output: an **auditable inquiry trail** — what the candidate asked, in what order, and how it shaped their conclusion.
